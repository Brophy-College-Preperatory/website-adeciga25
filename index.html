What is Pokémon?

Pokémon, short for "Pocket Monsters," is a popular media franchise created by Satoshi Tajiri and Ken Sugimori, first introduced in 1996 by Nintendo, Game Freak, and Creatures. The franchise revolves around fictional creatures called Pokémon, which humans, known as Pokémon Trainers, capture and train to battle each other. Players explore a world filled with diverse Pokémon species, each with unique abilities and traits. The central goal in the games is often to capture, train, and battle with Pokémon, aiming to become the Pokémon Champion. The franchise has expanded into an extensive range of video games, animated TV series, movies, trading card games, toys, and other merchandise, becoming a global cultural phenomenon. At its heart, Pokémon is about adventure, strategy, friendship, and the thrill of discovery, resonating with people of all ages.
